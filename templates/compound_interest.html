What Is Compound Interest?

Compound interest means earning interest not only on your original investment, but also on the interest that builds up over time. This creates exponential growth, especially in long-term investments.

The Compound Interest Formula

A = P (1 + r/n)^(nt)

Where A is the final amount, P is the principal, r is the annual interest rate, n is the number of times interest compounds per year, and t is the number of years.

Why It Matters

The earlier you start investing, the more powerful compound interest becomes. Even small contributions can grow significantly over decades.
